Favourite Books

Frank Herbert - Dune

I mean... where else do you start? Yeah, I'm a Beatles fanatic. Is it because of a misguided sense of loyalty to Liverpool? Hmmm, could be. Or, is it becuase I want to fit in? Maybe? OR... is it becuase they're the best band of all time? Definitely. In terms of impact, The Beatles are untouchable. They're one of thse very few acts were everything is as it should be - remove an element and it falls apart. At time of writing, I am currently waring out my 2021 remaster of Let It Be but that'll change soon enough. Favourite song? Well, right now, it's 'I've Got A Feeling' but as an all-time kind of thing, it'd probably be 'All You Need Is Love'. Also, Macca is the best Beatle. Don't @ me.

Isaac Asimov - Foundation Trilogy

If you were a massive Doors fan as a teenager and didn't think you were Jim Morrison (leather pants and all), then you're a liar. The Doors were the most influential part of my formative musical years. Doors fanatics are an odd breed. We kind of eat up everything Doors-related; know every word to every live show's interludes; slander the 1991 movie-biopic of the band (it doesn't feature ) sing every song in an embarrasing mock-Baritone fashion. We can spot each other a mile off - it's instinctual. I'd say my favourite album of theirs is The Soft Parade. It's a bit of a controversial pick but it's their most interestingly different work and it's a great, if sobering, commentary and counterpoint to the Swingin' 60s. Try it out!
